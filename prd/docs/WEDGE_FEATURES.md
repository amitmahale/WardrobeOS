# Wardrobe OS — wedge features

## The point

Do not compete head-on as “another closet app.” Compete as the wardrobe optimizer that is brutally useful.

## Wedge feature 1 — Buy Next Unlock Engine

### What it does
Ranks the best next purchase by the number and quality of outfit combinations unlocked.

### Why it matters
This is tangible. It changes behavior and saves money.

### MVP version
- candidate library of 20–40 archetypes
- deterministic simulation
- explanation text

### Future version
- retailer matching
- budget-aware live shopping links
- brand and fit preferences

## Wedge feature 2 — Underused Item Revival

### What it does
Finds items with low wear count and suggests high-confidence combinations around them.

### Why it matters
Makes the app feel valuable even after wardrobe setup.

## Wedge feature 3 — Occasion Coverage

### What it does
Quantifies how well the wardrobe supports contexts like casual, work, dinner, formal, travel, hot-weather smart casual.

### Why it matters
Turns vague closet anxiety into measurable gaps.

## Wedge feature 4 — Trip Capsule Builder

### What it does
Builds a minimal packing set that covers the maximum number of outfits.

### Why it matters
This is sticky and highly shareable.

## Wedge feature 5 — Duplicate Detector

### What it does
Finds clusters of near-duplicate functionally redundant items.

### Why it matters
Prevents waste and supports future “do not buy” features.

## Rollout order

1. Buy Next Unlock Engine
2. Outfit Lab
3. Underused Item Revival
4. Occasion Coverage
5. Pack Planner
6. Duplicate Detector
